Gemini AI · Narrative Analysis

Where the narratives diverge

All three media ecosystems reported the same IAEA statement by Director General Grossi — making this event a rare convergence point. However, they diverge sharply on one question: who caused the blackout?

Russian state media (TASS, RT) attributed the outage to a Ukrainian military strike on Energodar — a claim not independently verified by any external source. Ukrainian media focused on the operational context of Russian occupation without directly addressing the IAEA investigation's cause. Western outlets treated attribution as an open question, anchoring coverage in the IAEA process rather than either side's claim.

The structural asymmetry is notable: Russia controls the plant, yet frames itself as victim of Ukrainian sabotage. Ukraine reports intense frontline activity (317 engagements, 8,359 drones) but describes all outcomes as defensive wins. Western media — constrained by access limitations — relied on institutional sources (IAEA, embassies) rather than ground reporting.
